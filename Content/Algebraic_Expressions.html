An algebraic expression is a combination of terms separated by addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.

 

What is term?

   - A term is a single mathematical expression. It may be a number or a variable or both.

   - The expression 2 + y has two terms: 2 which is a number and y which is a variable.

 

Numbers are constant. It is always has a fixed value.

Variables are not constant and does have a fixed value. It is generally denoted by letters (a, b, x, y etc.).

 

Examples:

   - 15 is a variable. If you say "I have 15 dollar in my wallet", we exactly know how much you have.

   - x is a variable. If you say "I have x dollar in my wallet", we don't know how much you have. Is it 5 dollar or 500 dollar? We won't know unless you specify what x is equivalent to.

 

10, 2 , 22 and all the numbers are constant, and a, b, c, d, x, y, z and all the letters are variable.

But,

  • 3y is not constant. It is still a variable. 3y is 3 times y (3 x y). It is like multiplying two numbers, except we don't know what the actual value of y is.
  • 5a is also not constant. Remember, when a number and a variable are multiplied, it will be variable.

 

Example:

  • 6 × 5     It is 30    => constant times constant is constant
  • 4 × a     It is 4a    => constant times variable is variable
  • a × b     It is ab    => variable times variable is variable

 

Examples on Algebraic expression:

  • 2 + x - y      It is an algebraic expression with three terms.
  • 4y      It is an algebraic expression with one term.

One more thing. 4y is a term and 4 is a coefficient.

Coefficient: It is a number placed before the variable in an algebraic expression.

Example

  • 3x2      3 is a coefficient
  • 5x2 + 4x      Both 5 and 4 are coefficients

Like Terms and Unlike Terms:

Like terms have the same variables and powers. The coefficients do not need to be same.

Unlike do not have the same variables or powers.
