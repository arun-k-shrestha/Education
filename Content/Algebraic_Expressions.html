An algebraic expression is a combination of terms separated by addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.

 

What is term?

   - A term is a single mathematical expression. It may be a number or a variable or both.

   - The expression 2 + y has two terms: 2 which is a number and y which is a variable.

 

Numbers are constant. It always has a fixed value.

Variables are not constant and doesnot have a fixed value. It is generally denoted by letters (a, b, x, y etc.).

 

Examples:

   - 15 is a constant. If you say "I have 15 dollars in my wallet", we exactly know how much you have.

   - x is a variable. If you say "I have x dollars in my wallet", we don't know how much you have. Is it 5 dollars or 500 dollars? We won't know unless you specify what x is equivalent to.

 

10, 2 , 22 and all the numbers are constant, and a, b, c, d, x, y, z and all the letters are variable.

But,

  • 3y is not constant. It is still a variable. 3y is 3 times y (3 × y). It is like multiplying two numbers, except we don't know what the actual value of y is.
  • 5a is also not constant. Remember, when a number and a variable are multiplied, it will be variable.

 

Example:

  • 6 × 5     It is 30    => constant times constant is constant
  • 4 × a     It is 4a    => constant times variable is variable
  • a × b     It is ab    => variable times variable is variable

 

Examples of Algebraic expression:

  • 2 + x - y      It is an algebraic expression with three terms.
  • 4y      It is an algebraic expression with one term.

One more thing. 4y is a term and 4 is a coefficient.

Coefficient: It is a number placed before the variable in an algebraic expression.

Example

  • 3x2      3 is a coefficient
  • 5x2 + 4x      Both 5 and 4 are coefficients

Like Terms and Unlike Terms:

Like terms have the same variables and powers. The coefficients do not need to be the same.

Example

  • 2x, 3x, or 10x
  • 3x2 , 10x2, or x2    Remember that 1x2 and x2 are the same thing. Example: 1 × x2 = x2 or 1 × 5 = 5.

 

Unlike terms are not like terms and do not have the same variables or powers.

Example

  • 2x, 3x2
  • 4x, 4y, 5a

 

We can only add or subtract like terms.

1) Adding like terms:

It's like adding numbers. If 4 + 5 = 9, then 4x + 5x = 9x

Example

  • 3a2 + 10a2 = 13a2
  • 5x4 + x4 = 6x4

2) Subtracting like terms:

It's like subtracting numbers. If 6-3 = 3, then 6y - 3y = 3y

Example

  • 12a2 + 2a2 - 10a2 = 4a2
  • 2y - 6y = -4y      Remember that taking away 6 from 2 is negative 4

You can divide or multiply both like terms and unlike terms:

3) Dividing like terms:

Example

  • 15x2 / 3x2 = 5     It is like dividing 15 by 3
  • 12y / 6y = 2y     It is like dividing 12 by 6
