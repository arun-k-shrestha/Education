What is an equation?

    An equation is a statement saying that two values/expressions are the same. The values/expressions are separated by an equal sign ( = ).

Examples:

  • 4 + 4 = 8      Here, 4 + 4 has some value which is equal to 8.
  • 5x + 2x = 7x      Here, 5x+ 2x is an algebraic expression and it is equal to 7x which is also an algebraic expression. They are separated by an equal sign.
  • 2x2 + 3x2 = 5x2

More example:

  • 5 + x = 8      This is also an equation. We will see how it is true.
  • 9 - x = 2

Starting with 5 + x = 8.

It means 5 plus some number is equal to 8. Instead of saying some number, we can call it x or y or any other variables. Just by looking at the equation, we can tell that x is equal to 3. Because 5 + 3 = 8.

Coming back to 9 - x = 2. If we take away some numbers from 9, we will have 2. So, is x equal to 7? Because, 9 - 7 = 2 ? Yes.

 

These were easy questions. When it comes to little complex problems, we need different ways to tackle the problem.

We can tackle the problems by adding, subtracting, multiplying, or dividing the variables or numbers.

 

The goal is to find the value of a variable. So, always try to isolate the variable from the numbers.

 

1) Subtracting:

    x + 6 = 10      Since the goal is to isolate x from the rest, we can get rid of 6 from the right
    We subtract 6 from both sides.
    x + 6 - 6 = 10 - 6.      This way we can cancel out the 6.
    x + 0 = 4      We got this because 6 - 6 is 0 and 10 - 6 is 4x = 4

When we add, subtract, multiply or divide numbers or variables, we need to do those on both sides. The equation needs to balance out. Think of it as a balance scale. If you reduce the weight on the left side of the scale, you should also reduce the weight on the right side of the scale to balance out.

Bonus:

    x + 10 = 15
    x + 10 - 10 = 15 - 10.      Subtracting 10 from both sides
    x = 5      And, 10 - 10 is 0 and 15 - 10 is 5

Remember, x + 0 is 0. In fact, any number added with the 0 will be the same number. 6 + 0 = 6.

 

2) Adding:

    x - 5 = 10
    x - 5+ 5 = 10 + 5      Adding 5 on both sides. This way, we can get ride of 5.
    x = 15      And, 5 + 5 is 0 and 10 + 5 is 15

Let's take the same example:

    x - 5 = 10      Instead of adding 5 lets subtract 5 from both sides.
    x -5 - 5 = 10 - 5
    x - 10 = 5      We wanted to get rid of 5, but we got -10 instead. So, subtracting didn't work in this case.

If you see a positive number, subtract the number from both sides. If you see a negative number, add the numbers on both sides.

More example:

    10 - x = 20.      Since x is negative, let's make it positive first by adding the x on both sides
    10 - x + x = 20 + x      Here, - x plus x is 0
    10 = 20 + x
    Now we can get rid of 20 by subtracting 20 from both sides
    10 - 20 = 20 - 20 + x
    -10 = x      10 minus 20 is - 10. Taking away 20 from 10 is more negative.
    So, x = -10
