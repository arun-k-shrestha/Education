Order of Operations

Page

Operations mean adding, subtracting, multiplying, dividing, etc. So, the order of operations is a set of rules to solve a mathematical expression in the correct order.

When we see a problem like this: 2 x 4 - 3, should we multiply 2 and 4 or subtract 3 from 4?

The right way is to multiply 2 and 4 and then subtract later:

    2 x 4 - 3
    = 8 - 3
    = 5

The order of operations is:

    Parentheses,
    Exponents,
    Multiplication or Division,
    Addition or Subtraction

In the previous example, we multiplied first and then subtracted because multiplication has higher order than subtraction.
